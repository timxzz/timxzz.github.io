Publication

During the summer of 2015, I did an internship as an Research Assistant in the Advanced Processors Technologies(APT) Research Group, the origin of the world's first stored-program computer, in the School of Computer Science, University of Manchester. I worked with Dr. Dirk Koch, and investigated partial reconfiguration using Altera FPGAs. As a result, we wrote a paper:

A Partial Reconfiguration Controller for Altera Stratix V FPGAs

The International Conference on Field-Programmable Logic and Applications (FPL) 2016

"The paper presents details and the flow for implementing partial reconfiguration using Altera FPGAs, as well as a study on configuration bitstream sizes and configuration speeds for various resource and bounding-box aspect ratio variants. The results are used to build a partial reconfiguration controller that is featuring a lightweight but effective bitstream decompression module for greatly improving configuration speed on a DE5-net board."
